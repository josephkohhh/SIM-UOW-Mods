Gibbs Reflective Cycle

Reflection = Thinking about experience to learn (learning through experience)

it helps with:
– Better decision-making
– Better leadership

6 Stages of Gibbs’ Cycle
- Description
- Feelings
- Evaluation
- Analysis
- Conclusion
- Action Plan


Stage 1: Description
State what happened in a simple, factual way. 
Give context, mention who was involved, and describe what you did—without adding opinions or judgments

Stage 2: Feelings
Describe your thoughts and emotions before, during, and after the situation. 
This shows self-awareness and helps deepen reflection.

Stage 3: Evaluation
Identify what went well and what didn’t. Highlight both strengths and weaknesses by looking at positive and negative aspects of the situation.

Stage 4: Analysis
Explain why the situation happened. Explore underlying reasons, consider influencing factors, and connect to relevant theories for deeper understanding.

Stage 5: Conclusion
State what you’ve learned from the experience. Highlight key takeaways and what you would improve or do differently next time.

Stage 6: Action Plan
Outline what you will do next time. Suggest specific, practical actions to improve your performance or outcome.
