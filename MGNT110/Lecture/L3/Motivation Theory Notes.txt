Theory of Motivation 
- Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs (1970)
- Self-Determination Theory (2000)

Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs (1970)
- It suggests that ppl are driven by 5 levels of hierachy Needs.
- Ppl will focus on lower levels first and once those levels are satisfied,
- Ppl will move up to higher level needs 

1. Physiological Needs (base of the pyramid)
- To satisfy basic survival—things like food, water, sleep, and shelter. 
  Survival comes first.

2. Safety Needs: Security & Stability
- To secure personal safety, financial security and health first.
  Ex. Having a stable home or a secure job. 

3. Belonging: Social connections, love, affection, and intimacy.
- After safety, social needs become important. Humans yearn connections and
  a sense of belongingg. (friendship/relationship/family)
  Ex. Feeling accepted and loved helps fulfill this stage

4. Esteem: Respect and Recognition
- Ppl seek respect and recognition from others. This build up their 
  self esteem (confidence) as they feel valued and competemt   

5. Self-Actualization: Personal growth, creativity (top of the pyramid)
- Ppl aims to realize their full potential by becoming the the best version
  of themselves. 


Self-Determination Theory (2000)
- It suggests that individual motivation is determined by  
  - Intrinsic Motivation
  - Extrinsic Motivation

Intrinsic motivation comes from internal factors like self-expression, interest and enjoyment. 
- learning a language cuz u genuinely find interest 
- learning coding cause u like solving problems

Extrinsic motivation comes from external factors like rewards or punishments
- studying hard to get good grades or scholarship
- working part time job to earn money
- complete assignment to avoid failing module
- exercising to gain external validation/approval 


Intrinsic = “I do it because I enjoy it”
Extrinsic = “I do it because I get something from it (or avoid something)”
