1. Mangament is a mindset and framework
- It is set of assumptions, concepts, values and Practices


2. Managerialsim 
- A belief that management knowledge is powerful and important
  and that managers know the best way to run organisations


3. Organisations as purposive goal-oriented tools
- organisation must be arranged and coordinated so it can achieve
  its goals


4.  Managing involves sensemaking and framing
- sensemaking: 
  - trying to understand what is happening
  - interpreting a confusing situation
  - deciding what sth means

Managers often face uncertainty so they must make sense 
of market changes, staff problems, conflict, new tech, crises

Framing actions in order to accomplish objectives
- interpreting reality and influencing how others understand reality
  - ppl have their own unique biased views and own interpretations
  - we have our own mental model 
  - what we see is based on our personal interpretations based on our 
    expectations


Mental model
Pros:
- Enable us to think fast and act quickly
- More accurate mental models increase performance

Cons:
- wrong assumptions
- reject info that does not automatically fit


Managers operate in uncertainty not clarity 
- Unclear problems
- Conflicting goals
- Lack of resources
- Role ambiguity
- Uncertain outcomes


Cons: 
 Bounded Rationality
 human are not perfect rational decision makers. it is wrong to
 assume that managers are always logical, perfect decision makers

Humans have cognitive bias
1. too much data -> quickly filter what is impt -> may miss out impt details
2. confusing ambiguity -> fill gaps by assuming -> may lead to false beliefs 
3. fast pace -> too quick conclusions -> make poor decision (may be a one off error)
4. overload with data, simplifying too much -> few specifics (miss out impt details) -> error & bias (poor decision)


Management is not just doing tasks. Not just managing people.
- It is an ongoing thinking process of interpreting reality and influencing others

3 key Processes
1. Sensemaking: interpret
   - applying various mental models to respond to our environment
2. Sensegiving: influence
   - use certain mental models to frame a dominant way of practising in organisations
   - ex. this is a crisis, we must act fast
3. Sensebreaking: challenge
   - this models are challenged and reformed due to new knowledge, failures, crisis
   - ex. after covid, remote work becomes common


Summary:

Changing societal paradigms have implications for
managing and organising
• Flow and structure of managing and organizing entails
people, practices and processes and structures.
• In managing we deal with complexity, ambiguity, value
dilemmas, political pressures and paradigm shifts reflected
in changing nature of organisations, global conditions
economic contexts.
• Managers respond to such complexity, pressures and
paradigm shifts through processes of:
1. Sense making: interpret
2. Sense giving: influence
3. Sense breaking: challenge
