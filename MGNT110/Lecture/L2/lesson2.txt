Managing Individuals - understanding ppl in organisation

The perceptual process (SSOIR)
- stimulus
- selection
- organisation
- interpretation
- response

Ex.
- stimulus (alex is quiet)
- selection (notices alex is quiet, ignores alex's strong report)
- organisation (manager categorises behaviour, quiet employees = disengaged, active speakers = high performers)
- interpretation (intepret that alex is not motivated/not leadership material)
- response (exclude alex from key products, gives fewer opportunities, lower performance rating)


3 aspect of psychology
- perception 
- values 
- personality theories 

Perception
Perception are schemas at work
- schemas are set of cognitive constructs developed through social interactions that organize thoughts,
  feelings and attention
  - person schemas (confident speakers = competent employees)
  - role schemas (good employees shd speak up in meetings)

Common errors in perception
- stereotyping (“Quiet employee = low performer")
- self-fufilling prophecy (Manager gives fewer chances → Alex performs worse → confirms belief)
- halo effect (If Alex spoke well once → assume always good)
- devil effect (If Alex was late once → assume unreliable)
- attribution (Manager thinks: “Alex is lazy” Instead of: “Alex is new and still learning)
  - fundamental
  - self-serving bias
  - cognitive dissonance 

Values
A set of beliefs and goals that serve as guiding principles in one’s life
- core values dont change easily
  - if u value honesty, u apply it at work, school, r/s

Values have personal component and social component and they are not always aligned
- personal component (what u expect)
- social component (what society expect)


Personality
Personality = how you typically think, feel, and behave over time

Personality theories
- Trait theory
- Humanist Theory
- Socio-cognitive theory

Trait theory
- the big 5 model
  - openess: creative vs conventional 
  - Conscientiousness: organised vs careless
  - Extraversion: outgoing vs quiet
  - Agreeableness: cooperative vs competitive 
  - Neuroticism: emotionally unstable vs stable

Humanist Theory
Self-actualisation:
- personal growth
- fulfilment
- meaning in life and work

Good organisations (Humanist approach):
• provide training and development
• encourage growth
• give meaningful tasks
• support employee well-being
Poor organisations:
• focus only on output
• ignore employee development
• Result: burnout /disengagement /high turnover

Socio-cognitive theory
Behaviour is shaped by the interaction between personal factors, behaviour, and the environment (a loop):
• Environment influences behaviour
• Behaviour influences environment
• Personal beliefs influence both


Trait theory → what you are
Humanist theory → what you can become
Socio-cognitive theory → how you interact with situations


Positive Psychology
• encouraging employee strengths
• building resilience
• creating supportive environments
• promoting well-being

Result:
• higher motivation
• better performance


PERMA Model
5 elements of well-being:
- Positive emotion (gratitude) = recognising employees
- Engagement (finding and using strengths)  = challenging but achievable work
- Relationships  (develop connection) = good team culture
- Meaning (purposeful work) = meaningful roles
- Accomplishment (realistic goals and plans) = performance recognition
