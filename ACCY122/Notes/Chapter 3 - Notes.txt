Types of Transaction
- External transaction
  - Involve the biz and outside parties (customers, suppliers, banks, government, etc.)
  - Ex. Bought inventory from a supplier on credit, Paid rent to a landlord, Sold goods to a customer for
    cash, borrowed money from a bank 

- Internal transaction
  - Happen within the biz itself, with no outside party involved
  - Ex. Using office supplies, Depreciation on equipment
  - Xfering cash from petty cash to the main cash account

- Non-Transactional Events
  - Sth happened, but no actual accounting transaction happened yet, so nth is recorded
  - Ex. Signing a contract to buy equipment nxt mth, Market value of land rises, but no sale occurs,
    Hiring a new employee but not paid salary yet

*Dont be confused with accrual basis assumption since non-transactional events are basically just 'promises'
but no transaction happened yet.

Accounting periods
- Periodic progress of the entity is divided into time periods of equal length

Interim statements
- Statements prepared for external users before the end of the annual period

Accounting Cycle
- The steps and procedures, culminating in the preparation of financial statements


Accounts
• Asset
  - cash at bank
  - accounts receivable (AR)
  - debtors
  - GST outlays
  - prepaid expenses
  - land, buildings, vehicle, plant, equipment

• Liability
  - unearned income
  - loan
  - mortgage payable
  - accounts payable
  - GST payable and receivable

• Equity
  - capital
  - drawings or withdrawals

• Income
  - [name] services/fees revenue

• Expense
  - [name] expense 


Accounting Cycle:
1. Source document (invoice, PO, credit card slips, cash register tape) - daily
2. General Journal - daily
3. General Ledger - updated daily
4. Trial Balance - period-end (monthly, quarterly, year-end)
5. Financial Statements - SOE, Income Statements, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow Statement (same as trial bal)

*1 to 4 are primarily for internal users and 5 are mainly for external users

*Journal = records transactions, Ledger = stores accounts

A Chart of Accounts (COA) is a list of all account titles and numbers found in the general ledger
- Chart of Accounts = list of accounts
- General Ledger = detailed balances in those accounts
