Cash Basis
– Income is recorded when cash is received. 
– Expenses are recorded when cash is paid. 
– It does not recognise income when goods are sold or services are performed on credit.
– It is simple to operate.

Ex. U dont record as expense or income until payment is completed

Accrual Basis (Accrual Basis Assumption)
- Income is recognised in the period in which the expected inflow of 
economic benefit can be reliably measured.
– Expenses recognised when the consumption of benefits can be reliably measured.

Accounting has adopted the Accrual Basis Assumption

- Under the cash basis, profit is the excess of cash inflows from revenues over cash outflows for expenses.
- Under accrual accounting, profit is the excess of recognised revenues over recognised expenses.


The need for adjusting entries:
– Period in which cash is paid or received does not coincide with 
period in which expense and income are recognised.
– Some accounts must be adjusted on the last day of the accounting 
period to correctly recognise income and expenses not reflected in 
cash receipts or payments.

2 categories for adjusting entries
- Deferrals:
  - the expenses paid in advance (called ‘prepaid expenses’) or 
    revenues received in advance (called ‘unearned revenues’).
- Accruals:
  - the recognition of expenses incurred but not yet paid for 
    (called ‘accrued income/expenses’).


1. Prepaid Expenses (You paid first, but haven’t used up the benefit yet.)
   - Paid 1 year of rent for 12k (1mth used up)
     - DR Rent Expense +1,000
     - CR Prepaid Rent -1,000
2. Unearned Revenue (You received cash first, but haven’t earned it yet.)
   - 1mth of unearned revenue becomes earned
     - DR Unearned Revenue -100
     - CR Service Revenue +100
3. Accrued Expense (Expense incurred, but not yet paid)
   - Employees earned $2,000 wages by month-end, but payment will be made next week.
     - DR Wages Expense +2,000
     - CR Wages Payable +2,000
4. Accrued Income (Income earned, but not yet received.)
   - You provided consulting services worth $500, but the client hasn’t paid yet.
     - DR Accounts Receivable +500
     - CR Service Revenue +500 
5. Depreciation 
   - DR Depreciation Expense +2,000
   - CR Accumulated Depreciation -2,000


note:
drawings is an contra-equity account dr + / cr -
accum dep is an contra-asset account dr - / cr +
