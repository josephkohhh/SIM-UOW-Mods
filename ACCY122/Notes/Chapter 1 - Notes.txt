Accounting is a service activity
- to provide and interpret financial info to assist decision making
- used in biz, govt, charities, non-profits

Accounting is the process of identifying, measuring, recording and communicating economic info
- identification: observing economic events
- external transaction: economic events between 1 entity and another entity
- measuring must take place before the effects of transactions can be recorded
- recording provides history of economic activities of a particular entity

Accounting Process 
1. Identification (transactions)
2. Measurement (quantification in money terms)
3. Recording (classification, summarization) 
4. Communication (accounting reports -> analysis & interpretation) 


Users of accounting information
- Internal users
  - Managers
  - Owners
  - Employees
- External users
  - Investors (Retail/Institution)
  - Banks/creditors
  - Government (ATO)
  - Suppliers
  - Customers
  - Public 


2 broad branches of accounting
- Management 
- Financial

Financial Accounting (FA)
- Records past transactions fairly and accurately
- Follows strict accounting standards (IFRS/GAAP)
- Shows financial performance & position 
- For external users 

Managerial Accounting (MA)
- Focuses on planning & control
- Used for future decisions (budgeting, pricing, expansion)
- Flexible, no strict rules
- For internal users 


2 broad type of financial statements
- Special Purpose Financial Statements (SPFS)
  - Customized reports 
  - Prepared for specific users or specific needs
- General Purpose Financial Statements (GPFS)
  - Income statements, balance sheets, etc
  - For broad external users (investors, creditors, regulators)

*FA can have SPFS but usually focus more on GPFS
*MA does not have GPFS as it is prepared for external users
