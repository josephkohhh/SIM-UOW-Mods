Financial accounting vs Managerial accounting

Financial Accounting
- Records past transactions fairly and accurately
- Follows strict standards (IFRS/GAAP)
- Shows financial performance & position 
- For external users (shareholders)

Managerial Accounting
- Focuses on planning & control
- Used for future decisions (budgeting, pricing, expansion)
- Flexible, no strict rules
- For internal decision-making (managers)


Financial Accounting formula 
Assets = Liabilities + Equity


Assets
- Resources controlled by a biz from past events that are expected bring future benefits
- 2 types: 
  - Non Current Assets (nCA)
    - long term: > 1 year
      - buildings, machinery, equpiment, vehicles
  - Current Assets (CA)
    - short term: < 1 year
      - cash, inventory, accounts receivable


Liabilities 
- Present obligations of a biz arising from past events, expected to result in an outflow of resources.
- 2 types:
  - Non Current Liabilities (nCL)
    - due for repayment: > 1 year
      - long term bonds, mortgage loan
  - Current Liabilities (CL)
    - due for payment: < 1 year
      - creditors


Owner’s Equity
The owner’s claim on the biz after deducting liabilities from assets.
- capital invested by owner
- retained earnings (profits kept in biz)


Revenue
Income earned from normal business activities.
- Sales of goods
- Service income


Expenses
Costs incurred to generate revenue.
- Rent, salaries, utilities, cost of goods sold


Profit
- The surplus after total revenue exceeds total expenses.
- Profit = Total Revenue − Total Expenses
