These are inventory valuation methods in Principles of Accounts (POA), used to calculate Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and Closing Inventory when purchase prices change.

1. FIFO (First In, First Out)

Oldest goods are sold first.
Goods bought first are assumed to leave inventory first.

Formula (for COGS):
COGS = Cost of oldest units sold

Closing Inventory:
= Cost of newest units left

Summary:

Sell old stock first
Ending inventory uses latest prices
2. LIFO (Last In, First Out)

Newest goods are sold first.
Goods bought last are assumed to be sold first.

Formula (for COGS):
COGS = Cost of newest units sold

Closing Inventory:
= Cost of oldest units left

Summary:

Sell new stock first
Ending inventory uses oldest prices
3. WAVCO (Weighted Average Cost)

(Also called Average Cost Method)

All units are given an average cost.

Formula:
Average Cost per Unit=
Total Units Available
Total Cost of Goods Available
	​


Then:

COGS:
COGS=Units Sold×Average Cost per Unit

Closing Inventory:
Closing Inventory=Units Left×Average Cost per Unit

Summary:

Use average cost for every unit
No oldest/newest distinction
Quick memory trick
FIFO = First In First Out → oldest cost
LIFO = Last In First Out → newest cost
WAVCO = Weighted Average → average cost
