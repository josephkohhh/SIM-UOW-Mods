Inventory = goods a biz owns for sale

Ex.
- A phone shop’s inventory = phones, chargers, cases
- A grocery store’s inventory = food products on shelves
- A clothing store’s inventory = shirts, pants, shoes

Basically inventory = stock


When inventory is sold, it becomes Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) - an expense

Ex. 

- Buy inventory for $50
- Sell for $80

Revenue = $80
COGS = $50
Profit = $30 


Perpetual vs Periodic
- Perpetual and Periodic are 2 systems for tracking inventory

Perpetual Inventory System (continuous updating)
Every time inventory moves, records are updated immediately
- Buy goods → Inventory account increases
- Sell goods → Inventory decreases
- COGS is recorded right away

Ex.
- Buy 10 units for $5 each 
- Inventory = $50 
- Sell 2 units = $10
- Inventory becomes $40
- COGS = $10

Common with modern POS systems (supermarkets, large businesses).

Periodic Inventory System (updated periodically)
Periodic = update only at intervals (end of month/year)
- Purchases go into Purchases Account (not Inventory directly)
- Inventory is not continuously updated
- At the end, do a stock count and calculate: COGS=Beginning Inventory+Purchases−Ending Inventory

Ex.
Beginning inventory = $1000
Purchases = $500
Ending inventory count = $300
COGS = 1000 + 500 − 300 = $1200



Conclusion:
More common today: Perpetual inventory system
- Used by many modern biz cuz software tracks stock automatically




1.  Perpetual System journal entries

Buy inventory on credit ($500)
Dr Inventory            500
   Cr Accounts Payable       500

Sell goods for $800 cash, cost was $500
1. 
Dr Cash                 800
   Cr Sales Revenue         800

2.
Dr Cost of Goods Sold   500
   Cr Inventory             500


2. Periodic System journal entries

Buy inventory on credit ($500)
Dr Purchases            500
   Cr Accounts Payable       500

Sell goods for $800 cash
Dr Cash                 800
   Cr Sales Revenue         800

At end of period, after stock count, COGS is calculated:
COGS=Beginning Inventory+Purchases−Ending Inventory

Then adjustments are made.
